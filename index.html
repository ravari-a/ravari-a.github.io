Amirhossein Ravari

PhD Candidate · Electrical & Computer Engineering, Northeastern University

Boston, MA

ravari.a [at] northeastern [dot] edu

I am a PhD candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Northeastern University, advised by Prof. Mahdi Imani in the Imani Lab. My research focuses on reinforcement learning, inverse reinforcement learning, and human–AI collaboration in complex decision-making environments — with applications spanning cyber defense, healthcare, and disaster response.

My recent work has appeared in IEEE Transactions on Artificial Intelligence, L4DC, ACC, Asilomar, and ICASSP. I have also worked on adversarial inverse learning conditioned on human psychological factor models, and on optimal inference of hidden Markov models from expert-acquired data.

Before Northeastern, I completed my M.Sc. and B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering at Sharif University of Technology. Most recently, I was a Data Scientist at Walkky, where I worked on multimodal language models integrating ECG signals, echocardiograms, and clinical reports for unified diagnostic representations.

I am currently looking for Summer/Fall 2026 research internships in machine learning — across applied research, ML engineering, and quantitative finance. Feel free to reach out.
